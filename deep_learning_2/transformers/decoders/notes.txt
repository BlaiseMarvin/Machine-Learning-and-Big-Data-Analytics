Prompt Engineering:
- u can get wildly different answers depending on how you phrase a question, with the while spending some time experimenting with various prompts
- Different choices of words, give a few examples, suggest a character to imitate (e.g. you are a friendly.....)
- Specify the output format and style, list pitfalls to avoid and so on.

- Can even write a program to try out many possible combinations of prompt variants and eval them to find the best prompt for your task
- There are even automatic prompt optimization (APO) techniques such as prompt tuning where a few learned embeddings are preprended to the inputs

- can also breakdown complex tasks into multiple subtasks and prompt the llm once for each subtask - the output of one prompt feeding into another - prompt chaining

- related technique - chain of thought prompting encourages the llm to think step by step rather than jumping to rhe first answer. 
we can ask the llm explicitly (e.g. proceed step by step) or give it a few examples of the type of stp-by-step answer expect/ CoT prompting increases
the reliability of the output, especially for reasoning tasks. 
- pushing further we can make the llm explore multiple reasoning branches: approach is called tree-of-thoughts. works by asking the llm to reason step by step,
and at each step we make it generate multiple options 

- LLMs - hallucinations: to avoid this, one of the most important techniques is to add relevant context to the prompt on the fly. (RAG)
